History of Global Terrorism(1970-2017)

Between 1970 and 2017, global terrorism went through significant transformations, marked by evolving tactics, new groups emerging, and unprecedented attacks that reshaped world politics. The rise of ideological, political, and religious extremism contributed to a wide range of terrorist activities that have had far-reaching implications on international security, global cooperation, and counterterrorism policies.

Key Events in the History of Global Terrorism (1970-2017)

1970s - The Rise of Political and Revolutionary Terrorism

The 1970s saw an increase in political and revolutionary terrorism, particularly in Europe and Latin America. The most notable groups were the Red Army Faction (RAF) in Germany, the Weather Underground in the U.S., and the Palestinian Liberation Organization (PLO). Hijackings, bombings, and kidnappings became common tactics.

  • Red Army Faction (Wikipedia)
  • Weather Underground (Wikipedia)
  • The PLO: A Brief History

1980s - Islamic Terrorism and State-Sponsored Violence

The 1980s marked the rise of religiously motivated terrorism, particularly in the Middle East. Iran's 1979 revolution led to the formation of Hezbollah in Lebanon. Al-Qaeda also emerged towards the end of the decade. The U.S. embassy hostage crisis in Tehran (1979-1981) and the bombing of U.S. Marines in Beirut (1983) were defining events of this decade.

  • Iran Hostage Crisis (Wikipedia)
  • 1983 Beirut barracks bombing (Wikipedia)
  • Council on Foreign Relations Terrorism Tracker

1990s - The Globalization of Jihad

The 1990s saw the global expansion of Islamist terrorist networks. The formation of al-Qaeda in 1988 under Osama bin Laden aimed at waging a global jihad against the West. In 1993, al-Qaeda orchestrated its first attack on U.S. soil with the bombing of the World Trade Center in New York. The 1990s also saw increased violence in Africa and the Middle East.

  • 1993 World Trade Center bombing (Wikipedia)
  • Al-Qaeda (Wikipedia)
  • Council on Foreign Relations Terrorism Tracker

2000s - The September 11 Attacks and the War on Terror

The September 11, 2001 attacks by al-Qaeda on the U.S. marked the deadliest act of terrorism in history. The attacks led to the U.S. War on Terror, including military actions in Afghanistan and Iraq. Counterterrorism policies were significantly restructured globally, with an emphasis on intelligence and military operations.

  • 9/11 Memorial & Museum
  • September 11 attacks (Wikipedia)
  • Council on Foreign Relations Terrorism Tracker

2010s - The Rise of ISIS and Regional Instability

The rise of ISIS (Islamic State of Iraq and Syria) in the 2010s posed a new challenge in global terrorism. ISIS's brutal tactics, territorial ambitions, and ability to recruit globally through social media resulted in widespread terror attacks across Europe, the Middle East, and Africa. The 2015 Paris attacks and the 2016 Brussels bombings are notable events during this period.

  • Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (Wikipedia)
  • Council on Foreign Relations Terrorism Tracker
  • The New York Times: ISIS

2017 - ISIS Declines but Terrorism Remains a Threat

By 2017, ISIS lost most of its territorial holdings in Iraq and Syria, but it remained a significant threat. Terrorist groups around the world continued to adapt, employing new tactics such as vehicle ramming attacks and lone-wolf terrorism. The global fight against terrorism continued, with efforts to prevent radicalization and disrupt networks.
